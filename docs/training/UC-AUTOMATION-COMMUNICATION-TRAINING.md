# UC Automation Communication Training

**Purpose:** Help Ross refine the way he explains UC, contact centre, CRM, Microsoft 365, automation and AI to sales colleagues, customers, managers and vendor partners.

**Goal:** Become calm, clear and commercially compelling when explaining what modern UC can do and why it matters.

---

# 1. The Core Communication Problem

I understand the technical potential deeply because I can actually build these solutions.

I can see how UC, contact centre, CRM, Microsoft 365, APIs and AI can be connected to remove bottlenecks, automate work and transform how businesses operate.

The challenge is that I do not always have the language ready when speaking to non-technical people.

That gap can cause frustration.

The aim of this document is to build the vocabulary, phrases, stories and practice habits that help me explain the value clearly.

The objective is not to sound clever.

The objective is to make the value obvious.

---

# 2. My Core Message

## Short Version

> I help enterprise customers turn voice, contact centre, CRM, Microsoft 365 and AI into automated business workflows.

## Even Shorter Version

> I connect customer conversations to business action.

## Sales-Friendly Version

> I help customers remove the manual work that happens before, during and after calls.

## Customer-Friendly Version

> A customer call should not end when the caller hangs up. It should update the CRM, create the task, notify the right team and prepare the follow-up.

## Executive Version

> Modern UC is becoming part of the business automation layer. It is no longer just about calls and meetings; it is about turning conversations into action, insight and revenue.

---

# 3. The One Big Idea

The call is no longer the end of the workflow.

The call is the trigger for the workflow.

Everything else comes from this.

Examples:

- a sales call should trigger a CRM update
- a support call should trigger a ticket update
- a complaint should trigger an escalation
- a missed call should trigger a follow-up
- a contact centre interaction should trigger insight
- a meeting should trigger actions
- a conversation should trigger the next step

# 6. The Simple Explanation Framework

When explaining anything, use this structure:

## 1. Current problem

> The customer conversation happens in one system, but the work happens somewhere else.

## 2. Hidden cost

> People have to manually update CRM, send messages, remember follow-ups and create reports.

## 3. New possibility

> Modern UC platforms expose APIs, events and AI capabilities, so we can connect the conversation to the workflow.

## 4. Business outcome

> Calls can automatically create tasks, update records, notify teams and give managers visibility.

## 5. Simple summary

> The call becomes the trigger for action.

# 7. Daily Practice Routine

Spend 10 minutes per working day practising one idea out loud.

Use this format:

```text
The problem is...
The reason it matters is...
What we can do now is...
The business outcome is...
In simple terms...
```

## Example

```text
The problem is that customer calls often happen outside the CRM.

The reason it matters is that salespeople forget notes, managers lose visibility and follow-ups get missed.

What we can do now is connect Zoom Phone to HubSpot and Microsoft Teams so the call automatically creates a record, summary, task and notification.

The business outcome is less admin, better follow-up and cleaner CRM data.

In simple terms, the call updates the business instead of relying on the user to do it manually.
```

Use this when someone asks what I am working on.

# 10. 60-Second Explanation

Use this with salespeople or managers.

> A lot of customers have the same problem: their conversations happen in one place, but their business process happens somewhere else.
>
> A sales call happens in Zoom or Teams, but the CRM still needs updating manually. A support call happens in the contact centre, but the ticket notes still need typing. A customer complains, but the escalation still depends on someone remembering to send a message.
>
> What I’m building shows how UC, CRM, Microsoft 365 and AI can be connected so those actions happen automatically.
>
> The value is less admin, better follow-up, cleaner CRM data, better reporting and a better customer experience.

---

# 11. Executive Explanation

Use this with senior customers.

> The strategic opportunity is to stop treating UC as standalone communications infrastructure.

> Every customer conversation contains operational data, commercial intent and follow-up actions. Historically, that value has been lost because the phone system, CRM, contact centre and collaboration tools were disconnected.

>
> The result is a more efficient business, not just a better phone system.

# 14. Wavenet Money Opportunity

The commercial opportunity for Wavenet is that UC is moving from voice-only delivery into workflow transformation.

Traditional voice projects are valuable, but they are often scoped around implementation:

- design the voice platform
- port numbers
- configure users
- build call flows
- migrate sites
- test and hand over

That work still matters, but it can become commoditised. Many voice engineers can deliver a voice-only project if the design is clear and the customer requirements are traditional.

The new money is in connecting communications to the systems that run the business.

Examples:

- Zoom Phone connected to HubSpot
- Zoom Contact Center connected to CRM and Microsoft Teams
- Mitel modernisation connected to reporting and workflow improvement
- Teams Direct Routing connected to business process and compliance requirements
- AI summaries connected to tickets, tasks and follow-up
- call events connected to dashboards, alerts and customer records

The key commercial shift is:

> Voice-only projects are implementation revenue.  
> UC automation projects are business transformation revenue.

For Wavenet, this creates higher-margin, stickier, more strategic work.

It also protects Wavenet from being seen as just another UC reseller or telecoms provider. If customers believe Wavenet can connect voice, contact centre, CRM, Microsoft 365 and AI into business workflows, Wavenet becomes harder to replace.

# 16. Frustration Reframe

When I feel frustrated, it usually means:

> I am trying to explain a high-resolution idea to someone who does not yet have the same context.

That is not their fault.

It is my job to build a better bridge.

The bridge is:

1. problem
2. cost
3. example
4. outcome

Example:

> The problem is reps forget to update CRM.  
> The cost is poor visibility and missed follow-ups.  
> The example is a Zoom call automatically creating a HubSpot note and Teams alert.  
> The outcome is faster follow-up and cleaner pipeline data.

# 17. Questions to Ask Instead of Over-Explaining

## Customer Questions

- What happens after a customer call ends?
- Who updates the CRM?
- What gets missed?
- Where does the process slow down?
- What do agents have to type manually?
- What do managers wish they could see?
- Which follow-ups depend on someone remembering?
- Which systems should know that the call happened?
- What would you automate first if it was easy?

## Sales Questions

- Has the customer complained about CRM hygiene?
- Have they mentioned after-call work?
- Are they interested in AI but unsure where to start?
- Are they reviewing contact centre?
- Are they trying to modernise Mitel?
- Are they using HubSpot, Dynamics or Salesforce?
- Do they use Teams heavily?

Good questions reduce frustration because they make the other person reveal the pain in their own words.

# 19. Communication Rules

1. Lead with pain, not technology.
2. Use one clear example before explaining the architecture.
3. Say `business workflow` more than `API`.
4. Say `remove manual work` more than `integrate systems`.
5. Say `visibility` more than `data`.
6. Say `follow-up` more than `automation`.
7. Stop after making the point.
8. If someone wants technical detail, they will ask.
